What we do

Federal Government Relations

CHG continuously monitors developments in Washington, DC that are important to clients, communicates significant proposed actions to clients, and helps develop and communicate client positions to congressional and Administration staff and principals. We maintain on-going contact with Federal agencies and committees that correspond to the Federal agenda and remain alert to all areas which could have a beneficial or detrimental impact on clients. When clients travel to Washington, we coordinate all Washington activities; CHG schedules and prepares clients for meetings with their congressional delegation, authorizing and appropriations committee chairs and staff, senior executive and legislative branch officials and their State office, as appropriate.

CHG communicates with clients on a daily, weekly and monthly basis, as appropriate, on the results of our efforts to:
  • Monitor legislation and agency rulemaking
    • Identify ongoing or discretionary grant opportunities, including opportunities such as TIGER grants, and assist CAT staff prepare grant applications
  • Participate in coalition meetings
  • Attend Congressional mark-ups and hearings
  • Meet with congressional and committee offices, Federal agency and Administration officials in order to advance client’s interests
  • Represent clients at public interest group meetings in DC, and
  • Track pertinent Federal policy and monitor appropriations and authorizing legislation with regular reports to clients

Winning Federal Grants

CHG assists in obtaining and expediting Federal grants. Working in conjunction with Federal agencies, the firm advises clients on how to satisfy planning, procurement, environmental, Buy America, labor, ADA, and other Federal grant requirements. The firms also provide technical and management advice during all phases of project development, helping navigate complex Federal processes, educating and advising local public officials, local boards/councils and client staff. The firm will helps maintain and advance relationships with key Federal agency staff at both the regional and headquarters level.

Drafting and Research

CHG provide technical and strategic help to clients and delegation staff to draft any needed legislative language, congressional testimony, floor statements, and letters, and to offer advice on how to respond to requests for information. In addition to helping with any written briefing materials, we would also assist in preparing and presenting oral communications, whether they are formal testimony before committees or informal briefings with small groups or individuals. We will serve as an extension of client staff, as well as that of the Members’ staff, ensuring they have the research, legislative, and technical support, and staffing resources necessary.

Washington Update July 6, 2015

FY 2016 Transportation, Housing and Urban Developments (T-HUD) Appropriations Bill

The Senate Committee on Appropriations approved the $55.65 billion FY 2016 THUD appropriations bill, making the measure available for Senate floor consideration. A number of amendments were offered to increase funding for Amtrak, TIGER grants, New Starts, and Passenger Train Control implementation, but none were adopted.  A partisan battle in the Senate over sequestration may prevent the THUD bill from getting Senate floor time.  If that occurs, it is likely that USDOT programs, as well as many other federal agency programs, would be funded through a Continuing Resolution at last year’s levels as of the October 1 start of the new fiscal year. The full House previously approved its version of the bill on June 10. Click here for additional info.

 

Senate Committee Approves Six-Year Transportation Bill

The Senate Environment and Public Works Committee unanimously approved a six-year surface transportation reauthorization, called the DRIVE Act (S. 1647). The bill proposes to spend $278 billion on transportation projects over the six-year period, or about $46 billion a year, which is slightly above current MAP-21 spending levels. However, the bill doesn’t contain a proposal to fill the looming Highway Trust Fund shortfall. Similar to a mechanism devised for the WRRDA bill last year, the Senate’s bill contains the “next best thing to earmarks,” authority for FHWA to submit a list of “projects of high importance” to the House and Senate authorizing committees, which would then approve $350 million worth of those projects annually. For more: DRIVE Act Press Release and Summary, Senators Inhofe and Boxer’s Statement, or Secretary Anthony Foxx Statement. At some point in July, Congress will have to pass another extension of MAP-21 since the latest short-term bill expires on July 31.  One big difference with the next extension is that Congress will have to identify at least $10B in new revenue or spending cuts in order to fund the highway and transit programs through the end of the calendar year. 

 

Aviation

This week House Transportation and Infrastructure Committee Chairman Bill Shuster (R-PA) is expected to introduce the House version of a bi-partisan FAA reauthorization bill which will propose privatizing the air traffic control system.  The bill may be marked up in committee as early as July 9.  

 

Online Sales Tax Bill Introduced by Congressman Chaffetz

Rep. Jason Chaffetz introduced the Remote Transactions Parity Act (RTPA), H.R. 2775, his legislative response to the Marketplace Fairness Act (MFA). MFA passed the Senate by a final vote of 69-27 in May 2013. RTPA’s policy changes align closely with MFA’s, authorizing the collection of remote sales tax by 45 states with statewide sales taxes and Alaska. Enactment of an online sales tax bill would generate at least an estimated $23 billion in new tax revenue for states nationwide, benefitting local governments as well.

 

Paid Leave Analysis Competition

Labor Secretary Tom Perez announced a new $1.25 million grant competition under the Paid Leave Analysis program, which is intended to support research and analysis needed to explore, develop, implement and/or improve paid family and medical leave programs at the state and municipal levels. Applications are due by July 15. For more:  Lead on Leave Website, Press Release, or Grant Opportunity.

 

Broadband in Public Housing

HUD is seeking input, in advance, on a new “Digital Opportunity Demonstration” that it will announce in more detail, later. The demonstration is being designed to test the effectiveness of collaborative efforts by government, industry, and nonprofit organizations to accelerate broadband internet adoption and use in HUD-assisted homes. Approximately 20 HUD-assisted communities, selected from across the country, are anticipated to participate in the demonstration. For more: Digital Opportunity Demonstration.

 

Tax Extenders May be Addressed by Autumn

Speaking to The Wall Street Journal CFO Network, House Ways and Means Chairman Paul Ryan addressed the annual ritual of “tax extenders,” saying, “We want to do this as early as possible, in the fall.” These provisions include: the alternative fuel tax credit and empowerment zone tax incentives. 

 

SCOTUS Supports HUD Fair Housing Rule

In the case of Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs v. The Inclusive Communities Project, Inc., the Supreme Court ruled 5-4 that HUD may now move forward on enforcing its Affirmatively Furthering Fair Housing Rule, which guards against certain forms of housing discrimination, such as predatory lending, exclusionary zoning, and development policies that limit affordable housing. For more, click on Supreme Court Ruling,HUD Secretary Castro’s Statement, Attorney General Lynch’s Statement, White House Statement, or Affirmatively Furthering Fair Housing Rule.

 

Proposed Rule to Expand Overtime Pay

The White House announced that the Labor Department would propose a long-awaited overtime rule that is estimated to raise wages for nearly 5 million workers as early as 2016. The proposed rule will raise the salary level under which most workers qualify for overtime pay whenever they work more than 40 hours in any given week, from $23,660 to $50,440. For more, click on White House Fact Sheet or Labor Department Overtime Website.

 

 

FTA Announces Improved Safety Testing

FTA announced a notice of proposed rulemaking that would improve the process for testing the safety
and reliability of new transit buses funded with federal dollars. Public comments are due by August 24. For more: FTA Transit Buses Proposed Rule.

 

Core Values: Why American Companies Are Moving Downtown

Last month, Smart Growth America released a study called “Core Values: Why American Companies are Moving Downtown.” The study identified 500 companies across the country that moved to downtown from the suburbs, or moved and expanded within downtown, between 2010 and 2015. The study reports that 247 of the companies it identified move from the suburbs, 92 opened a new downtown office, 73 relocated within downtown, 41 expanded downtown and 20 consolidated a suburban and a downtown location. For more: Core Values: Why American Companies are Moving Downtown.

NEA FY16 Our Town Grant Program, The Skills for Success Program, The Investing in Innovation Fund, NEH Media Projects Grants, and EPA Region 5 Wetland Program Development Grants.

 

U.S. Conference of Mayors 83rd Annual Meeting

 

Policies Adopted

More than 100 resolutions were adopted by the Mayors, covering a wide range of topics including My Brother’s Keeper, Ending Veterans’ Homelessness, Moving to Work, Transportation Reauthorization, Ending Sequestration, Climate Change, and Municipal Broadband, along with dozens of other subjects. For more: USCM Adopted Resolutions.

 

Speakers

President Barak Obama: On working with Mayors to achieve mutual policy goals, he said, “On America’s most important economic priorities… cities are not standing still. You are moving forward. So I want to say two things to you. Number one – thank you… Second, I also want to say don’t stop now, because we’ve still got a lot more work to do.” The President spoke about the progress that has been made on a wide range of topics, including climate change, My Brother’s Keeper, clean energy, the minimum wage, and high-tech manufacturing. He stressed the importance of promoting new infrastructure investments, boosting domestic programs, and ending homelessness. He ended by saying, “I’m a city guy,” and that no one’s zip code should determine their destiny. For more: President’s Remarks or President Obama Video.

 

Hillary Clinton: She spoke about her past work with Mayors, including her “dear friend” Tom Menino and “our friend” Joe Riley. She declared, “If I am president, America’s Mayors will always have a friend in the White House.” She believes that we need to institute “Flexible Federalism” that empowers and connects communities, and that Mayors are a vital part of the “Four Fights” at the center of her campaign: (1) Building an economy for tomorrow, not yesterday, (2) Strengthening America’s families, (3) Harness all of our power, our smarts, and our values to continue to lead the world, and (4) Revitalizing our democracy back here at home. For more: Secretary Clinton’s Remarks or Clinton Video.

 

Mayor Rahm Emanuel: Chicago Mayor Rahm Emanuel spoke to the Conference about the importance of education, saying that there are two economies: one for skilled labor, one for unskilled. While education at all levels should be promoted, he emphasized the need to invest in preschool and the post-High School years as particularly important. For more: Mayor Emanuel Video.

 

Mayor Ralph Becker: Salt Lake City Mayor Ralph Becker, who serves as President of the National League of Cities, thanked the Conference for closely working with NLC on many issues, in line with the theme of his Presidency, “Connecting.” For more: Mayor Becker Video.

 

Task Forces and Seminars

Jobs, Education, and the Workforce Labor: Secretary Thomas Perez spoke to the Committee about the “skills super-highway” needed to boost workforce development. He talked about the need to make community college more affordable, the need to focus on “career pathways” more than individual jobs, and the benefits of Registered Apprenticeships. Tacoma Mayor Marilyn Strickland talked about the link between transportation and job opportunities.

 

Children, Health, and Human Services: Michael Botticelli, Director of the White House Office of National Drug Control Policy, spoke to the Committee about the national opioid epidemic and outlined federal resources available to help Mayors combat this pressing issue on-the-ground, especially in the Northeast and Midwest, where the epidemic is most pronounced.

 

Hunger and Homelessness Task Force: The Task Force: Chaired by Santa Barbara Mayor Helene Schneider, focused on work being done in her city and others, like Albuquerque and Anaheim, to reduce homelessness. Amy Sawyer with the U.S. Interagency Council on Homelessness, talked about the progress made, and work left to be done, since the Council’s comprehensive “Opening Doors” strategy was released in June 2010.

 

Mayors and Police Chiefs Task Force: White House Office of Intergovernmental Affairs Director Jerry Abramson joined Fresno Mayor Ashley Swearengin and Gary, IN Mayor Karen Freeman-Wilson to discuss the findings of the Report of the President’s Task Force on 21st Century Policing and the importance of improving relationships between law enforcement and their communities.

 

Veterans Affairs Task Force: Patrick O’Brien, Director of the Defense Department’s Office of Economic Adjustment, advised Task Force members to contact his office if they hear of a pending base closure, or contract or workforce reduction in their community. His office continues to follow BRAC discussions in Congress closely.

 

Technology and Innovation Task Force: Megan Smith, U.S. Chief Technology Officer, highlighted the work that cities are engaging in around the country to promote tech literacy, including Code Louisville. She called on cities to lift up IT employees that might be “buried” in the lower levels of government, and include them in the conversation at the executive levels. She expressed concern about the increasing “vendorizing” of tech at all levels of government – local, state, and federal.

 

Ports and Exports Task Force: Steven Stockton, Director of Civil Works for the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, spoke to the Task Force about the importance that inland waterways and barges play in promoting U.S. exports, especially since the enactment of WRRDA.
